Date : 02/03/2023


"Insidious" is a horror movie directed by James Wan and written by Leigh Whannell. The movie follows the story of Josh (Patrick Wilson) and Renai (Rose Byrne), a couple who move into a new house with their three children. Strange events start to occur in the house, including unexplained noises, doors opening and closing on their own, and ghostly apparitions. When their son Dalton (Ty Simpkins) falls into a coma-like state and is unable to wake up, Josh and Renai begin to suspect that there may be something supernatural going on.

The movie's strongest aspect is its atmosphere. The eerie music, dimly-lit scenes, and jump scares create a sense of tension and dread throughout the movie. The special effects are also well-done, particularly the scenes in the "Further", a purgatory-like realm where the ghosts reside.

The performances of the cast are solid, with Patrick Wilson and Rose Byrne giving emotional and convincing performances as the distressed parents. Lin Shaye is also great as Elise, a psychic who helps the family understand and confront the supernatural forces at play.

However, the movie does have its flaws. The pacing of the movie can be slow at times, particularly in the first act. The dialogue can also feel stilted and forced, particularly during scenes where characters are explaining the mythology of the supernatural elements.

Overall, "Insidious" is a solid horror movie that effectively creates a creepy atmosphere and delivers some genuine scares. While it may not be perfect, it's still a worthwhile watch for fans of the horror genre.
